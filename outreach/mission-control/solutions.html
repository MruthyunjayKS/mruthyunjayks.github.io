👨‍🏫 How to Run the Room (Facilitation)

As the teacher, you are the Flight Director. You act as the interface between the students and the computer simulation. To create a true NASA Headquarters atmosphere, enforce the Silence Protocol.

  • 1. Distribute Packets: Give each student a specific role (NAV, OPS, SCI, ENG) and their printed Dossier.
  • 2. Enforce Silence: Tell them the communication satellites are down. They must solve their math individually on paper.
  • 3. Pass the Notes: Students must physically write their answers on "Data Packets" (scratch paper) and hand them to the Chief Engineer.
  • 4. The Launch: The Engineer brings the final numbers to your desk. You type them into the Simulation on the projector for everyone to watch.

🛒 The "Golden Paths" (Procurement)

The Engineer has $100M. The Ares and Falcon engines are "traps" (they have failure percentages). To guarantee a 100% win, the Engineer MUST figure out how to afford the $50M Atlas V Engine while still buying the exact upgrades needed for the SCI and NAV team's choices.

🚀 Path 1: The Safe Route (Simplest) $90M
  • Route B (Safe) + Site A (Flat)
  • Engine: Atlas V ($50M)
  • Tank: Medium ($20M) - Required for Route B distance!
  • Life Support: Standard ($20M)
  • Upgrades: None needed.
🚀 Path 2: The Direct Express (Fastest) $90M
  • Route A (Asteroids) + Site A (Flat)
  • Engine: Atlas V ($50M)
  • Tank: Small ($10M)
  • Life Support: Standard ($20M)
  • Upgrades: Auto-Repair Bot ($10M) - Required to survive Asteroids!
🚀 Path 3: The Ice Hunter (Max Science) $100M
  • Route A (Asteroids) + Site B (Ice/Uneven)
  • Engine: Atlas V ($50M)
  • Tank: Small ($10M)
  • Life Support: Standard ($20M)
  • Upgrades: Repair Bot ($10M) + Landing Thrusters ($10M)
  • Note: This is the ONLY way to safely land on the ice and get water. If they take Route B, they can't afford the Thrusters.

⚠️ Navigating "Unseen Anomalies"

Mid-way through the simulation, the screen will suddenly flash RED and trigger an "Unseen Anomaly". The computer will pause and wait for an input.

FACILITATION TIP: When the alarm sounds, shout "QUICK HUDDLE!" Break the silence rule. Tell the teams they have exactly 60 seconds to work together, solve the math problem on the screen, and yell the answer to you. If you type in the wrong number, the simulation instantly fails.

Safe Mode Anomalies (Basic Math)

  • Panel Debris: Subtraction (100 - 25 = 75)
  • Course Drift: Multiplication (50 x 2 = 100)
  • Cabin Heating: Addition (70 + 35 = 105)

Adventure Anomalies (Algebra)

Variables are randomized every game, but the formulas remain the same:

  • O2 Leak: Reserve - (Rate x Hours)
  • Power Rerouting: (Total Output - LS Draw) / 4
  • Velocity: Current Vel x 0.75

💬 The Debriefing

Once the rocket lands (or crashes), bring the class back together to discuss the Systems Engineering aspect of the game.

  • The Domino Effect: Ask the Engineer how the Navigator's choice (Route B) affected their budget (forced to buy a $20M tank, leaving less money for a safe engine).
  • Communication Breakdown: Did the Scientist realize they needed Thrusters for the Ice Cap, but fail to tell the Engineer to buy them?
  • The Cost of Safety: Ask the class if they think real NASA engineers struggle with "Budget vs. Safety" when building rockets like Artemis or Apollo.
