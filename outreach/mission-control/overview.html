Simulation Briefing

MISSION CONTROL

Operation: "The Great Silence" // Ares I Rescue Mission

🌍

The Scenario

Solar Cycle 25 has peaked. Massive X-Class flares have fried all global GPS and communication satellites. We are flying blind.

The Mars Colony is stranded. We are operating from a shielded bunker using a hard-line connection to launch a "Dumb Rocket" (analog tech).

Computers are down. All flight calculations must be done manually by your team.

🔒

Operational Rules

  • 🔇
    SILENCE PROTOCOL Audio comms are dead. Verbal communication is strictly forbidden.
  • 📝
    PAPER LAN Data must be written on "Data Packets" (slips of paper) and physically routed to the Engineer.
  • 💰
    HARD BUDGET Total mission cost must not exceed $100,000,000.

THE MISSION SQUAD

🧭

NAV

Navigator

Analyzes star maps. Calculates travel distance, Delta-V, and chooses the flight path.

📦

OPS

Operations

Calculates Life Support payload mass and safety buffers based on flight parameters.

🔬

SCI

Scientist

Analyzes Martian surface data. Determines landing sites and critical entry physics.

🔧

ENG

Chief Engineer

Team Lead. Receives data packets, manages the $100M budget, and inputs final codes.

Mission Timeline

PHASE 1

Briefing & Intel

Teams review Dossiers. Calculate individual mathematical data points.

PHASE 2

Transmission

Teams write "Data Packets". Router physically delivers them to ENG.

PHASE 3

Procurement

ENG buys Engine, Tank, & Upgrades. Verifies the team's math.

PHASE 4

Launch Simulation

Director inputs data. Teams huddle to solve mid-flight anomalies.
